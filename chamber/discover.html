The reopening of Notre-Dame

On December the 8th, Notre-Dame will reopen after the devastating fire in 2019. With the reopening a five year plan was put in place to revamp the surrounding of the cathedral into a green make-over. This operation will start in autumn 2025 and will cost 50 milion of euros. Some of changes will be transforming the underground parking into a visitor space or turning the Seine's quay into a new promenade. This project also aims to create 1,800 square meters of green space with 160 new trees.

Photo competition:

Share your best photos of the Paris 2024 Games

You were in Paris this summer and captured unforgettable moments during the Olympic and Paralympic Games? Try to be among the 24 amateur photographers who will see their photo exhibited at the Hôtel de Ville as part of an exhibition, from December 20 to March 1, 2025. Registration for the competition is open from October 6 to 20!
To participate, you must be an amateur photographer, an adult and a Parisian. The photograph must be accompanied by a caption specifying the date it was taken as well as the exact location. Only one photograph can be submitted, it's up to you to choose it carefully!

Family outings to celebrate Halloween!

On All Saints' Day, come celebrate “Dia de los Muertos” at the Jardin d'Acclimatation!

On All Saints’ Day to celebrate Día de Muertos the Acclimatation's Garden will be in Mexican colors, for a traditional and musical festival. Skeletons, mariachis and Catrinas will invade the park’s paths to the rhythm of wild melodies. Thrill-seekers will not be left out with The Bewitched River and the mayan labyrinth!

Halloween Ghost Hunt at the Hôtel de la Marine

A few steps from the Tuileries Gardens, this architectural treasure opens its doors for a ghost hunt in its ceremonial rooms! Among the gilding, period furniture and works that inhabit it, children will have to spot the evil spirits hidden in the decor!

“Not even scared” at the Jardin des Plantes

For All Saints' Day, the menagerie at the Jardin des Plantes will take on the appearance of a laboratory from beyond the grave... Snakes, spiders, insects and rats are already getting ready to give little visitors goosebumps! These creatures will be in the spotlight through a series of activities. And for the Halloween atmosphere, an arachnologist will present these unloved animals to daring children on Thursday, October 24.
